Rheeya Uppaal

I am a Machine Learning Researcher at Goldman Sachs CoreAI, where I work on information extraction from text in the financial domain. I recently completed my Masters in Computer Science at UMass Amherst, where I worked in the Information Extraction and Synthesis Lab under Prof Andrew McCallum, and in the Information Fusion Lab under Prof Madalina Fiterau. My primary area of interest lies in developing algorithms in domains with low supervision, and making neural networks learn meaningful representations algined with human understanding. To this end, I have delved into Transfer Learning, Domain Adaptation, Few Shot Learning, Active Learning and the open ended world of Interpretability. Apart from this, I have worked on Deep Learning applied to NLP and Vision tasks, and have thoroughly enjoyed the experience.

I started my journey of research as an undergraduate, under the wonderful guidance of Prof Balaraman Ravindran at IIT Madras, where I worked on Object Tracking and Classification in video data. I was introduced to the world of Machine Learning by Prof Emma Brunksill at the OurCS Workshop at CMU.

I like to spend my free time reading anything under the sun, singing or playing my violin. I am also an amateur nature photographer, and never miss an opportunity to capture a nice shot. I have also taken up figure skating recently, and it is quickly becoming one of my favourite sports!
